Welcome

Mapping Teejop is a digital mapping project that guides users on an Indigenous walking tour of the UW-Madison campus. Created in partnership between the American Indian & Indigenous Studies Program and the Department of Geography’s Cartography Lab, Mapping Teejop provides an accessible and interactive experience for visitors to UW-Madison to learn about and explore the Ho-Chunk history and contemporary Indigenous presence on campus.

Tours

Mąįirakere Grounding

Mąįrakere is the first digital tour in the Mapping Teejop series. As such, it is meant to expose, orient, and “ground” students, faculty, staff, and visitors to the Ho-Chunk and Native history and presence of the University of Wisconsin.

As a land grant institution, the University of Wisconsin has a deep history and complex history with the Ho-Chunk Nation, a history that has often been deliberately erased, ignored, and overlooked.

Mąįrakere begins at one of the most iconic locations on campus, Bascom Hill. Departing from the Our Shared Future Marker, users will be guided over Bascom Hill, onto Observatory Drive, before ending at the Tree of Peace near Washburn Observatory.

Along the way, Mąįrakere provides content and context for users of Mapping Teejop to not only learn about Ho-Chunk and Native history and presence at the University of Wisconsin, a region long known as Teejop, but opportunities for critical and self-reflection.

Mąįrakere closely mirrors the First Nations Cultural Landscape Tour which provides a more in-depth and personal guided tour experience for those wanting to learn more and engage with trained guides. Together, Mapping Teejop and the First Nations Cultural Landscape Tour work to challenge erasure narratives, affirm Ho-Chunk history and presence, and to provide a more critical history of the University of Wisconsin.
