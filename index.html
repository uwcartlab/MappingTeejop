Grounding Grounding

Grounding is the first digital tour in the Mapping Teejop series. As such, it is meant to expose, orient, and “ground” students, faculty, staff, and visitors to the Ho-Chunk and Native history and presence of the University of Wisconsin.

As a land grant institution, the University of Wisconsin has a deep history and complex history with the Ho-Chunk Nation, a history that has often been deliberately erased, ignored, and overlooked.

Grounding begins at one of the most iconic locations on campus, the Memorial Union. Departing from Memorial Union, users will be guided up Bascom Hill, and eventually onto Observatory Drive, before ending at the Tree of Peace near Washburn Observatory.

Along the way, Grounding provides content and context for users of Mapping Teejop to not only learn about Ho-Chunk and Native history and presence at the University of Wisconsin, a region long known as Teejop, but opportunities for critical and self-reflection.

Grounding closely mirrors the First Nations Cultural Landscape Tour that provides a more in-depth guided tour option for those wanting to learn more and engage with trained tour guides. Together, Mapping Teejop and the First Nations Cultural Landscape Tour work to challenge erasure narratives, affirm Ho-Chunk history and presence, and to provide a more critical history of the University of Wisconsin.
